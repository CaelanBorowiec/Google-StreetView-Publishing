Imaging

Mounting the Gigapan to the Tripod

Firstly the Gigapan must be mounted to the tripod. To do this, gently screw the Gigapan's tripod mount down onto the screw threads of the tripod.

If your tripod has a smaller-sized mounting screw, you will need a 3/8" to 1/4" tripod thread adapter, like this one.

Mounting the Camera to the Gigapan

  1. Screw the tripod mounting plate onto the bottom of the DSLR. The plate is directional and the edge with the "Gigapan Systems" text should be pointing toward the screen.
  2. Insert the mounting plate into the camera rail and pull the locking lever. If the lever will not turn, make sure that the mounting plate is completely flat against the rail.
  3. Flip the small silver safety latch. This will prevent the locking lever from opening accidentally.
  4. Remove the camera strap to prevent tangling.
  5. Connect the trigger cable to the camera (Mini-USB to Gigapan and Canon trigger plug to camera).

Taking a 360° Panorama

  1. Set the camera to Full Manual shooting mode
  2. Set the exposure settings on the camera:
    • Appropriate white balance
    • High aperture value (ƒ22 suggested)
    • Low ISO value (~250 suggested)
    • Shutter speed as needed for exposure1
    • Lens focal length (11mm)
  3. Focus the lens on a point in the middle-distance, then set it to manual focus
  4. Using the Gigapan's controls, select 360 Panorama
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions

1 Shutter speeds slower than 2 seconds may require you to change the Gigapan's pause delay.

 

Tip: You can quickly start a new panorama using the previous settings by using Panorama Memory: Previous.
